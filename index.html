Research Interests

Bayesian nonparametrics and data privacy. My research focuses on proposing, studying, and applying Bayesian nonparametric models for single and regression density estimation, hypothesis testing, massive data, and imputation of erroneous and missing values. In addition, I work with Professor Jerry Reiter on developing differentially private procedures and synthetic data generators based on nonparametric approaches. My work has involved interdisciplinary efforts at the intersection of statistics, computer science, political science, environmental engineering, and psychometrics.
